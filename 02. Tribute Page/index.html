Linus Torvalds

Finnish software engineer (developer of the Linux kernel.)

Linus Torvalds

Time-line of Linus Torvalds

1969 - December 28: Linus Benedict Torvalds is born in Helsinki, Finland.

1981 - At age 11, Linus begins programming on his grandfather’s Commodore VIC-20, sparking his interest in computers.

1988 - Enrolls at the University of Helsinki to study computer science.

1991 - April: Purchases his first PC, an Intel 80386-based machine, and starts experimenting with its operating system. September 17: Releases the first version of the Linux kernel (version 0.01) to the public, initially as a hobby project. October 5: Announces Linux kernel version 0.02, marking the official birth of the Linux operating system.

1994 - March 14: Releases Linux kernel version 1.0, a stable and functional version of the operating system.

1996 - Completes his master’s degree at the University of Helsinki with a thesis titled "Linux: A Portable Operating System." Adopts the penguin mascot (Tux) as the official logo for Linux.

1997 - Moves to the United States to work at Transmeta Corporation, a startup developing energy-efficient CPUs.

1999 - Receives the 1999 EFF Pioneer Award for his contributions to open-source software.

2003 - Joins the Open Source Development Labs (OSDL), which later merges with the Free Standards Group to become the Linux Foundation.

2005 - Creates Git, a distributed version control system, to manage Linux kernel development after disagreements with proprietary tools like BitKeeper.

2010 - Receives the Millennium Technology Prize, one of the world’s most prestigious technology awards, for creating Linux.

2012 - Inducted into the Internet Hall of Fame for his contributions to open-source software and the internet.

2014 - Receives the IEEE Computer Society’s Computer Pioneer Award for his work on Linux.

2018 - Awarded the IEEE Masaru Ibuka Consumer Electronics Award for his contributions to open-source software.

2021 - Continues to oversee Linux kernel development, which powers millions of devices worldwide, from servers to smartphones (via Android).

2023 - Remains an influential figure in the tech world, advocating for open-source software and collaborating with the Linux Foundation.

Key Contributions Linux Kernel: The foundation of the Linux operating system, used in servers, supercomputers, embedded systems, and more. Git: A revolutionary version control system widely used in software development. Open-Source Advocacy: A pioneer in the open-source movement, promoting collaboration and transparency in software development.

Linus Torvalds is a visionary who revolutionized the world of technology through his creation of the Linux kernel and Git. His work has empowered millions of developers, organizations, and individuals to build, innovate, and collaborate freely. Linus’ commitment to open-source principles has not only shaped the software industry but also inspired a global movement toward transparency and shared knowledge. Despite his occasional bluntness and strong opinions, his contributions remain unparalleled, and his legacy continues to grow as Linux and Git remain foundational tools in modern computing.
